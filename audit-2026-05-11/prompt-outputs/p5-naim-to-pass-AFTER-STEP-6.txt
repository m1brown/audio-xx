RESPONSE:
Naim → Pass Labs is mainly a philosophical shift, not automatically a "better" move. Whether it counts as an upgrade depends on what you currently value about Naim's presentation and where you want to move.

**Naim.** Timing-domain emphasis through power-supply design and discrete circuit topology; tightly damped bass alignment in the integrated amplifiers. Listeners prioritising rhythmic momentum on rock, jazz, electronic material; less aligned for listeners prioritising spatial precision or tonal density.

**Pass Labs.** Class-A solid-state with low or zero feedback (Nelson Pass design lineage). Even-order harmonic content from class-A topology, contrasted with tube circuits. Listeners seeking tube-adjacent harmonic structure without tube maintenance. First Watt models pair with high-efficiency speakers; Pass flagship models drive a wide range.

**What you would likely gain (Pass Labs's strengths):**
• dense (tonally weighted), harmonically natural
• relaxed transient response without being slow

**What you would likely give up (Naim's strengths):**
• propulsive and forward, tightly defined bass
• transient definition emphasised

**It is more likely a real upgrade if:** you've been wishing for what Pass Labs prioritises — and Naim's strengths feel less important to you than they used to.

**It is more likely sideways (or the wrong move) if:** you actively value Naim's strengths in your current listening — moving to Pass Labs would trade them away for a different philosophy you may or may not prefer.

To answer this concretely, two things help:
• What about your current Naim setup do you most value — and what's been bugging you about it?
• What's the rest of the chain? The same amplifier change can read as upgrade or sideways depending on speakers and source.
